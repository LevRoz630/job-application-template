# AI Writing Patterns to Avoid in Cover Letters

## Words that scream AI
- delve, embark, foster, endeavor, beacon
- paramount, profound, meticulous, robust
- leverage, utilize, harness, unlock
- groundbreaking, cutting-edge, game changer
- tapestry, realm, journey, landscape
- resonate, underscore, shed light on
- bespoke, tailored, curated
- navigate, navigating complexities
- elevate, amplify, unleash

## Phrases to avoid
- "at the intersection of X and Y"
- "actionable insights"
- "I'm eager to / excited about / passionate about"
- "in today's fast-paced world"
- "it is important to note that"
- "designed to enhance"
- "unlock the potential"
- "revolutionize the way"
- "when it comes to"
- "in the realm of"

## Structural patterns
- Everything in threes (three examples, three adjectives)
- Equally sized paragraphs (real writing has varied paragraph lengths)
- Overly balanced sentences ("whether X or Y", "from A to B")
- Perfect parallel structure everywhere
- "Beyond the X, I also Y"
- Starting paragraphs with "Furthermore" / "Moreover" / "Additionally" / "Indeed"
- The contrastive reframe: "It's not X, it's Y"
- Formulaic intros: "In today's digital age..."
- Symmetrical pros/cons lists

## Formatting issues
- Using hyphens and em dashes, avoid them (use commas or separate sentences instead)

## Tone issues
- Too polished / no rough edges
- Every sentence feels "complete"
- No contractions (always "I am" never "I'm")
- Excessive hedging ("I believe", "I feel that")
- Generic enthusiasm without specifics
- Monotonous sentence length (all medium-length)
- No personality or humor

## What to do instead
- Write like you talk
- Be specific about what you actually want
- It's okay to sound a bit rough
- Use contractions
- Say things directly without preamble
- Include details only you would know
- Mix short and long sentences
- Add personal anecdotes or specific details
- Read it aloud - if it sounds weird, rewrite it
- Use fragments occasionally. Like this.
